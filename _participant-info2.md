# **Scottish Gaelic Electromagnetic Articulography Study: Participants Needed**

We are looking for native speakers of Scottish Gaelic to participate in a research study using electromagnetic articulography (EMA).


---
## **Who Can Take Part?**

  We are looking for **native speakers of Scottish Gaelic**.

 By _native speaker_, we mean:

- You learned Gaelic from one or more of your parents or caregivers during childhood.
    
- You continue to use Gaelic in daily life, for example with family, friends, or in your community.

---

## **About the Study**

We are conducting a study using [**electromagnetic articulography (EMA)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_articulography) to investigate the movements of the tongue, lips, and jaw during speech.

 Most articulatory data of this kind comes from widely spoken languages such as English and Mandarin Chinese. Gaelic is of interest because it includes some typologically uncommon sounds. By including Gaelic, we aim to contribute to the scientific understanding of speech production and ensure that minority languages are represented in the wider literature.

---

## **What Will Participation Involve?**

  
If you decide to take part:

- You will be asked to read aloud words and sentences in Gaelic while small sensors are glued on your tongue, lips, and jaw.
    
- These sensors are connected by thin wires to a recording system that tracks your articulatory movements in real time.
    
- The session may feel slightly unusual or awkward, but it is not painful.
    

  
Participants will receive a **small nominal payment ** in appreciation of their time.

[Here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pf_KpTWr4kw&t=613s) is a link to a Youtube video of researchers from the Yale Phonetics Lab carrying out an EMA experiment in Ghana.

---

## **Safety**

  

Electromagnetic articulography is a **biologically safe** and **non-invasive** method.

- There are **no documented adverse health effects**.
    
- The main discomfort is minor: the sensors are temporarily attached inside the mouth using non-toxic adhesive and may take a few minutes to get used to.
